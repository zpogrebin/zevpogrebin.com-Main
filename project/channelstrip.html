Channel Strip

A channel strip is a device used in the professional audio industry for recording and processing of sound. Channel strips generally consist of a microphone preamplifier, an equalizer, a filter, and other effects.

For my high school's senior capstone, I designed one of these devices. It has a transformer based mic preamplifier, a high pass filter, and a 4 band EQ. The preamp has continuous gain control from +20dB to +60dB. The channel strip's built in HPF can be engaged at 60Hz and 120Hz, and has a selectable slope of 12 and 18 dB per octave. The EQ section contains 2 bands of inductor based EQ and 2 bands of active EQ. Each band has 5 selectable frequencies and continuous gain control from -15dB to +15dB.

The design incorporates a hand engraved aluminum front panel and an analog VU meter. The circuitry is layed out on a custom PCB with through hole components.
